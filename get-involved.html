VOLUNTEER · 50 by year's end

Volunteers are the connective tissue.

Luna moves from 50 active volunteers in year one toward a structured volunteer corps in year two.

Every volunteer completes a short application, signs a code of conduct, and commits to a 60–90 day schedule. Volunteers who work directly with minors complete background checks.

Volunteer groups:

  • Artist volunteers and guest instructors
  • Workshop assistants and studio support
  • Event setup crew and hospitality
  • Social media and content volunteers
  • Fundraising ambassadors
  • Cleaning and reset crew
  • Student interns

TEACH A CLASS · Paid stipend

Local artists, paid.

Luna hires Wilmington artists as educators, performers, and cultural workers. Stipend is set by the board's artist payment policy and paid the week of the class.

You can propose a one-off workshop, a four-week series, a Luna Night performance slot, or a Rotating Method residency for one of the twelve disciplines.

What we look for:

  • A clear teaching idea and audience in mind
  • Wilmington roots — lived experience, not just a portfolio
  • Cultural humility and openness to absolute beginners
  • A reasonable supply list (we can usually source it)
  • Willingness to be paid as an independent contractor

Background checks are completed before any teaching that involves minors.

BOARD SERVICE · Two-year founding commitment

Help us govern.

The founding board is five to seven directors. Each brings either functional expertise, community credibility, or both.

Service is a serious commitment: quarterly meetings, committee work, financial responsibility, and full participation in setting up the institution properly from day one. Founding board members shape what Luna becomes.

Seats we're recruiting for:

  • Treasurer (financial oversight)
  • Secretary (compliance, records)
  • Artist representative
  • Education / youth representative
  • Fundraising / business representative
  • Community / parent representative

COMMUNITY PARTNERS

Highest-leverage growth.

Partnerships bring audience, credibility, and often in-kind support that would cost real cash to replicate. They are how a young nonprofit grows past its founder's network.

Natural early partners:

  • Wilmington public and charter school teachers
  • Public and parochial schools, after-school programs
  • Creative District Wilmington
  • Churches, faith communities, community centers
  • Local galleries, restaurants, music venues
  • Bartenders & service-industry networks
